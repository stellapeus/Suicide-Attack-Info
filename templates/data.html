The data in this JSON file tracks suicide attacks worldwide from 1982 to October 2020, offering a rich and comprehensive record of these incidents across multiple regions. It includes detailed information about each attack, such as the location, specific target, and the weapon used, as well as vital details about the attackers involved, including their identities, if available. The database compiles data from a variety of sources in different languages, ensuring a broad and diverse collection of over 4,800 attacks. For each entry, researchers can find key information such as the exact date of the attack, the group responsible (if known), the type of weapon employed, the number of casualties (both killed and wounded), and further information about the attackers, including gender and number of participants. This extensive resource is invaluable for researchers and analysts, helping them to track the evolution of suicide attacks over time, identify patterns in attack strategies, and assess the broader impacts of these events on societies and regions across the globe.
